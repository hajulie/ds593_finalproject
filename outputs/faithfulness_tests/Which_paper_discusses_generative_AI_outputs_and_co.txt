Answer:

Based on the sources provided, the paper discusses generative-AI outputs and copyright, and uses the case of ChatGPT, Bard, DALL·E, Ideogram, and Cowboy Riding a Rocket Ship as examples. The paper argues that generative-AI systems raise serious copyright issues, and that the unpredictability of AI-generated outputs makes it difficult to determine who should be liable for infringement. The paper also discusses the copyright analysis of these different stages of the generative-AI supply chain, including authorship, similarity, direct and indirect liability, fair use, and licensing. The paper concludes that there are connections between upstream technical designs and downstream uses, and that the complicated sociotechnical system of generative-AI systems must be analyzed in order to determine who bears responsibility for infringement.
